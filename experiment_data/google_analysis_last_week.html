This is typically used when IV (Implied Volatility) is low because, if IV increases at the first expiration (when the short option expires), then we can sell our long option for a higher price.

But I typically try to find specific cases when the spread is very tight, meaning that I pay a low premium and I can gain more or a high maximum return / cost. For example:

  • Sell GOOGL 305 Put May’26 for $2485 credit.
